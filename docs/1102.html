1102 - How do the different body structures of animals relate to their movements?

Different body structures of animals help them move in unique ways. Here are some examples:

  1. Floppy ears (Flying): Bats have thin, flexible ears that help them fly by catching wind and giving them balance.
  2. Fins (Swimming): Fish have fins that move back and forth to push water and swim.
  3. Strong back legs (Jumping): Kangaroos and frogs have powerful back legs that help them jump high and far.
  4. Webbed feet (Swimming and diving): Ducks and penguins have webbed feet that make them great swimmers. The webs help them paddle and dive underwater.
  5. Weak front legs and strong claws (Climbing): Cats and monkeys have weak front legs but strong claws that help them grip and climb trees.
  6. Long neck and strong legs (Running): Giraffes have long necks and strong legs that help them run fast, see predators from far away, and reach food in tall trees.

Each animal's body structure is adapted to its environment and helps it move in a way that allows it to survive and thrive.
