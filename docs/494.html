494 - How do zoos care for the animals that live there?

Zoos care for animals in several ways:

  1. Food and Water: They provide nutritious food and fresh water to each animal every day.
  2. Spacious Homes: Animals live in large enclosures that mimic their natural habitats, giving them space to roam and exercise.
  3. Veterinary Care: Zoos have veterinarians (animal doctors) to check animals' health, vaccinate them, and treat any illnesses or injuries.
  4. Cleaning and Hygiene: Enclosures are cleaned regularly to keep animals healthy and prevent the spread of diseases.
  5. Social Interaction: Many animals, like monkeys and lions, are kept in groups to give them social interaction and companionship.
  6. Enrichment Activities: Zoos provide toys, puzzles, and other activities to keep animals mentally and physically active, just like they would in the wild.

Overall, zoos work hard to create a comfortable and safe environment for the animals to live in.
