1404 - What is the current situation and future outlook of energy utilization in Taiwan?

In Taiwan, the current situation and future outlook of energy utilization can be explained briefly as follows:

Current Situation: Taiwan relies heavily on imported fossil fuels (like coal, natural gas, and oil) to generate most of its energy. Renewable energy sources like solar and wind power are increasing, but still make up a small part of the energy mix.

Challenges: 1. Heavy reliance on imported fuels affects energy security. 2. High greenhouse gas emissions contribute to climate change.

Future Outlook: Taiwan aims to increase the use of renewable energy and decrease reliance on fossil fuels. By 2025, the goal is to generate: 1. 20% of electricity from renewable energy. 2. 50% from natural gas. 3. Lower nuclear power and coal usage.

Key Plans: 1. Offshore wind farms to produce more clean energy. 2. Encouraging solar panel installation on homes and buildings. 3. Development of green technologies and smart grids.

Taiwan's energy future aims to be more sustainable, secure, and environmentally friendly.
