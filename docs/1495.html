1495 - How does nuclear power generation ensure radiation safety?

Nuclear power generation has many safety measures to protect people from radiation. Here are some of them:

  1. Thick Containment Walls: The nuclear reactor is surrounded by very thick, strong walls made of concrete and steel that can stop radiation from getting out.
  2. Cooling Systems: The reactor is cooled with water to prevent overheating and radiation leaks. The water is kept inside a separate system to prevent it from getting contaminated.
  3. Shielding: Thick layers of lead or concrete are used to block radiation from getting out of the reactor.
  4. Systems to Remove Waste: Special systems are in place to remove radioactive waste safely.
  5. Trained Staff: The people working at the power plant are trained to handle emergencies and follow strict safety rules.
  6. Regular Inspections: The power plant is inspected regularly to make sure it's working safely.
  7. Emergency Core Cooling System: This system can quickly cool the reactor in case of an emergency.

All these measures help to keep radiation levels low and protect people's safety.
