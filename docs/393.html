How do cultural practices affect ecological sustainability?

Imagine you live in a small village surrounded by a beautiful forest. The way your community interacts with that forest can affect its health. Cultural practices are like customs and habits that people follow.

Some cultural practices can harm the environment. For example, if a community believes in cutting down many trees to make a big celebration, that can harm the forest and its animals.

On the other hand, some practices can help the environment. For example, in some villages, people plant trees to celebrate special events or follow rules to protect certain animals and their habitats.

So, if a community makes choices that respect the environment, it can help keep the ecosystem healthy and strong.
