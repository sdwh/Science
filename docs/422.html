422 - How do gears work together to transmit motion?

Imagine you're riding a bike. When you pedal, it makes the gears turn. Here's how they work together:

  1. Teeth on gears: Gears have small teeth on them that fit into each other.
  2. Turning motion: When one gear turns, the teeth on it mesh with the teeth on another gear.
  3. Motion transfer: As the first gear turns, it makes the second gear turn too, but with a different speed or direction.
  4. Ratio: Depending on the size of the gears, the speed and direction of the motion can change. This is called a gear ratio.
  5. Chain reaction: Many gears work together in a sequence, forming a gear system. Each gear affects the next one, creating a change in motion.

For example, in a bike, the gear system helps change the speed of the wheels when you pedal. By switching gears, you can ride uphill faster, ride downhill safer, or cruise on flat ground.
