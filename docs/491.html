Science Common Sense

491 - What are the safety rules we need to follow in a science lab?

When working in a science lab, it's crucial to follow these safety rules:

  1. Wear protective gear: Wear goggles, gloves, and a lab coat to protect your eyes, skin, and clothes.
  2. Read instructions carefully: Before starting an experiment, read the instructions carefully to avoid accidents.
  3. Handle equipment with care: Use equipment, such as test tubes and Bunsen burners, carefully and with adult supervision.
  4. No eating or drinking: Don't eat or drink in the lab, as you might accidentally ingest chemicals.
  5. Wash your hands: Wash your hands with soap and water before and after experiments.
  6. Clean up spills: If you spill something, clean it up immediately to avoid slipping or chemical damage.
  7. No horseplay: Stay focused and avoid playing around in the lab, as it can be distracting and lead to accidents.
  8. Follow teacher instructions: Listen to your teacher and follow their instructions carefully.

Remember, safety is everyone's responsibility in a science lab. Be responsible and have fun learning!
