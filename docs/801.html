801 - How does climate change affect farming practices?

Climate change can affect farming in several ways.

  1. Changing weather patterns: More extreme weather like droughts, floods, and heatwaves can damage crops and make it harder to grow food.
  2. Shift in growing seasons: Warmer temperatures can cause plants to grow at different times, which can be confusing for farmers and animals.
  3. New pests and diseases: Changes in temperature and weather can bring new pests and diseases that can harm crops.
  4. Water scarcity: Less rainfall can make it harder for farmers to get the water they need for their crops.

To deal with these changes, farmers might:

  1. Grow different crops: Choose crops that are more resistant to extreme weather or changing conditions.
  2. Use new farming techniques: Like irrigation systems or special shades to protect plants from too much sun.
  3. Plant at different times: Adjust planting schedules to match the new growing seasons.

Farmers are adapting to climate change, but it's still a big challenge for them.
