How do carbon compounds, like carbon dioxide, affect the environment?

Carbon compounds like carbon dioxide (CO2) affect the environment in a big way.

  1. Trapping heat: CO2 is a greenhouse gas, which means it traps heat in the Earth's atmosphere, making the planet warmer. This is called the greenhouse effect.
  2. Climate change: Too much CO2 in the air causes climate change, leading to things like super-hot summers, really cold winters, and extreme weather events like hurricanes and droughts.
  3. Ocean acidification: When CO2 dissolves in the ocean, it makes the water more acidic. This can harm marine life, especially coral reefs and shellfish.
  4. Air pollution: CO2 can also combine with other pollutants in the air to create bad air quality, which can make people sick.

So, it's essential for us to reduce our carbon footprint by using less energy, planting more trees, and decreasing our reliance on fossil fuels.
