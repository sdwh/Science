1090 - What are the different functions or uses of substances or objects.

There are many different functions or uses of substances and objects. Here are some examples:

  1. Medicines: to cure or prevent illnesses (e.g., pain relievers, antibiotics)
  2. Food and Drinks: to give us energy and nutrients (e.g., fruits, vegetables, water)
  3. Materials: to build or create things (e.g., wood, metal, plastic)
  4. Cleaning Agents: to keep us and our surroundings clean (e.g., soap, detergent)
  5. Fuels: to power vehicles, homes, and machines (e.g., gasoline, electricity)
  6. Adhesives: to stick things together (e.g., glue, tape)
  7. Lubricants: to reduce friction and make things move smoothly (e.g., oil, grease)
  8. Insulators: to keep things warm or cool (e.g., thermoses, blankets)
  9. Toys and Games: to have fun and relax (e.g., balls, puzzles)
  10. Tools: to help us do tasks and projects (e.g., hammers, scissors)

These are just a few examples. Many substances and objects have multiple functions, and people can always find new and creative ways to use them.
