687 - How do we measure water quality?

Measuring water quality is like giving a health check to the water. There are a few main things we look at:

  1. pH level: This checks how acidic or basic the water is. If it's too acidic or basic, it can harm living things.
  2. Turbidity: This measures how clear the water is. If it's cloudy or murky, it can be a sign of pollution.
  3. Bacteria: We check for bad bacteria like E. coli, which can make people sick.
  4. Nitrates and Phosphates: These can cause too much algae growth, which can harm the environment.
  5. Temperature: We check if the water is too hot or too cold, which can affect the living things in it.

To check these things, scientists use special tools and equipment to collect and test water samples. Some of these tools include:

  • pH paper or a pH meter
  • Turbidity meter
  • Microscope to check for bacteria
  • Testing kits for nitrates and phosphates
  • Thermometer to check temperature

By checking these things, we can make sure the water is safe for drinking, swimming, and the environment.
