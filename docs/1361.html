1361 - How do human activities change the environment and impact the survival of other living things.

Human activities can harm the environment in several ways, affecting other living things. Here are some examples:

  1. Pollution: We release bad stuff like chemicals and waste into the air, water, and soil. This makes it hard for animals to breathe, drink, and live.
  2. Deforestation: We cut down too many trees for wood, paper, and space. This destroys homes for animals and affects the climate.
  3. Climate Change: We release greenhouse gases, like carbon dioxide, by burning fossil fuels. This leads to rising temperatures, melting ice caps, and extreme weather.
  4. Overfishing: We catch too many fish, leaving not enough for other animals to eat. This disrupts the ocean's food chain.
  5. Habitat destruction: We build cities, roads, and farms, taking away natural habitats for animals.
  6. Waste and litter: We throw away plastic and other trash, which harms animals who eat it or get trapped.

All these changes affect the survival of animals and plants. We must be kind to the Earth and reduce our negative impact to protect our planet.

You can help by:

  • Using reusable bags and water bottles
  • Turning off lights and electronics to save energy
  • Not wasting paper and water
  • Supporting organizations that plant trees and protect animals
  • Spreading awareness about the importance of protecting the environment
