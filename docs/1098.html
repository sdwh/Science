1098 - What are the differences in application properties that distinguish substances?

Imagine you have different kinds of materials like wood, paper, and metal. They all look and feel different because of their properties. Here are some key properties that can distinguish substances:

  1. Texture: How it feels when you touch it. Is it smooth, rough, or soft?
  2. Color: What color is it? Is it red, blue, or green?
  3. Size and shape: How big or small is it? What shape is it?
  4. Weight: How heavy or light is it?
  5. Rigidity: Is it hard or can it bend easily?
  6. Density: How tightly packed are the particles inside? Is it heavy for its size or light?
  7. Melting point: At what temperature does it melt or change from a solid to a liquid?
  8. Boiling point: At what temperature does it boil or change from a liquid to a gas?
  9. Conductivity: Can it conduct electricity or heat?
  10. Solubility: Can it dissolve in water or other liquids?

These properties help us identify and understand the characteristics of different substances.
