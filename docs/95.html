95 - What role do fungi play in the environment?

Fungi play a very important role in the environment. They are like nature's cleaners and recyclers. Here are a few things they do:

  1. Break down dead things: Fungi help break down dead plants and animals, turning them into fertilizer for new plants to grow.
  2. Help plants grow: Fungi form special relationships with plant roots, helping them absorb water and nutrients from the soil.
  3. Decompose pollutants: Some fungi can even break down pollutants like pesticides and heavy metals, making the environment cleaner.
  4. Create habitats: Fungi provide food and shelter for many animals, like insects and small mammals.

Overall, fungi are really important for keeping the environment healthy and balanced.
