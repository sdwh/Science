1703 - What are the applications of X-rays due to their higher energy and shorter wavelength than visible light?

Due to their high energy and short wavelength, X-rays have many useful applications. Here are some of the main ones:

  1. Medical Imaging: X-rays help doctors see inside the body. They can take pictures of bones and help diagnose broken bones, bone cancer, or lung problems.
  2. Airport Security: X-rays are used in baggage scanners to safely check for hidden items, like sharp objects or explosives, in luggage.
  3. Dental Check-ups: X-rays are used to see inside teeth and gums to detect cavities or other problems.
  4. Testing Materials: X-rays are used to check for cracks or defects in metal parts or other materials.
  5. Industrial Inspection: X-rays are used to inspect internal parts of machines or products, like welds or electronics.
  6. Space Exploration: X-rays are used to study the universe, including stars and galaxies.

These are just a few examples, but X-rays are really helpful in many areas because of their unique properties.
