What is the difference between a hurricane, typhoon, and cyclone?

The main difference between a hurricane, typhoon, and cyclone is the location where the storm takes place.

  • A hurricane happens in the Atlantic Ocean and the east coast of the United States, and the Caribbean Sea.
  • A typhoon happens in the Pacific Ocean near Asia and the west coast of the United States.
  • A cyclone happens in the Indian Ocean and the southern Pacific Ocean near Australia and India.

Other than the location, they all mean the same thing: a big, rotating storm with strong winds and heavy rain.
