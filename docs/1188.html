1188 - What harm can electrical appliances that produce electromagnetic waves and have functions cause?

Electrical appliances that produce electromagnetic waves can cause some harm. Here are a few examples:

  1. Headaches and dizziness: Some people can get headaches or feel dizzy from being near appliances like microwaves, computers, or TVs.
  2. Cancer risk: There is a possible link between long-term exposure to electromagnetic waves from appliances like cell phones and an increased risk of cancer.
  3. Sleep problems: Using electronic devices with screens (like phones, tablets, or laptops) before bedtime can affect sleep because they produce blue light.
  4. Hearing damage: Using earbuds or headphones turned up too loud can damage hearing.
  5. Interference with pacemakers: Some appliances can interfere with pacemakers or other implanted medical devices.
  6. Overheating: Some devices like laptops or smartphones can get very hot and cause burns.

To minimize harm, use electrical appliances responsibly:

  • Use them at a safe distance.
  • Follow the manufacturer's instructions.
  • Limit screen time.
  • Take breaks.
  • Turn them off when not in use.
