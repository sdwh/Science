1837 - How does soil and water conservation function in disaster prevention and reduction.

Soil and water conservation helps prevent and reduce disasters, like landslides and floods, in several ways:

  1. Prevents soil erosion: Plants and trees with strong roots hold soil together, so when it rains or floods, the soil doesn't get washed away.
  2. Protects watersheds: Covered soil absorbs rainwater like a sponge, slowly releasing it into rivers and streams, preventing flash floods.
  3. Maintains healthy vegetation: Green plants absorb excess rainwater, keeping water tables low, and reducing flood risks.
  4. Controls water flow: Created barriers like dams, or wetlands, slow down water flow, giving people time to escape or respond to emergencies.
  5. Provides early warnings: Soil and water conservation techniques can help detect signs of landslides or floods, allowing people to evacuate before disaster strikes.

So, by keeping soil healthy and managing water flow, we can prevent or reduce the impact of landslides and floods, keeping people and communities safe!
