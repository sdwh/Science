How do volcanoes affect the surrounding area?

Volcanoes can have both good and bad effects on the surrounding area.

The bad effects include: - Lava can destroy buildings and homes. - Ash can block sunlight, making it hard for plants to grow. - Ash can also contaminate water and make it hard to breathe. - Earthquakes and landslides can also occur near volcanoes.

The good effects include: - Volcanic ash can make the soil fertile and great for growing plants. - Volcanoes can create new land. - Minerals from the volcano can be used for medicine and other things. - Hot water from the volcano can be used for heating homes and making electricity.

Overall, volcanoes can be very powerful and have a big impact on the surrounding area.
