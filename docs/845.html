845 - How does exercise affect brain health?

Exercise is super good for your brain. It helps you stay smart, focused, and even happy. Here's how:

  1. More blood flow: When you exercise, your heart pumps more blood to your brain. This gives your brain cells the oxygen and food they need to work properly.
  2. New brain cells: Exercise actually helps grow new brain cells, especially in areas that help with learning and memory.
  3. Better mood: Exercise releases special chemicals called endorphins, which make you feel happy and relaxed.
  4. Sharp thinking: Exercise has been shown to improve focus, concentration, and even school performance.
  5. Less stress: Exercise can help reduce stress and anxiety, which can be bad for your brain.

So, regular exercise is like a superpower for your brain. It can help you stay smart, happy, and focused.
