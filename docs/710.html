How do logistics and supply chains work in transportation?

Imagine you ordered your favorite video game online. Here's how logistics and supply chains work to get it to you:

  1. Manufacturer: The video game is made in a factory (like a big warehouse where games are produced).
  2. Warehouse: The game goes to a warehouse where it's stored with many other games.
  3. Distribution: The warehouse sends the game to a Distribution Center (another big warehouse) where it's sorted with other packages going to the same area.
  4. Transportation: The game is put on a Truck, Train, or Plane to travel to a Delivery Center near you.
  5. Delivery: A delivery person (like a UPS or FedEx driver) picks up the game and brings it to your house.

This is called a supply chain, like a long chain of steps to get the game from the factory to your door. Logistics is like the management of this chain, making sure everything runs smoothly and the game gets delivered on time.

Think of logistics and supply chains like a well-oiled machine, working together to bring you the things you need and want!
