805 - How do farming practices impact the environment and biodiversity?

Imagine you have a garden with many flowers, trees, and animals. Farming practices can either help or hurt that garden. Here's how:

Bad impacts on the environment and biodiversity:

  1. Soil pollution: Using too many chemicals can harm the soil and make it hard for plants to grow.
  2. Water waste: Farming can use too much water, leaving less for other plants and animals.
  3. Deforestation: Cutting down trees for farmland can harm animals' homes and make the air dirtier.
  4. Loss of biodiversity: When we focus on one crop, we might miss out on other plants and animals that need space.

Good impacts on the environment and biodiversity:

  1. Sustainable farming: Growing many types of crops and using natural methods can help the soil and air.
  2. Using less water: Farming methods like drip irrigation can save water.
  3. Protecting forests: Leaving some trees can help keep the air clean and provide homes for animals.
  4. Supporting biodiversity: Planting a mix of crops can help many animals and plants thrive.

By choosing good farming practices, we can help protect the environment and keep the garden healthy!
