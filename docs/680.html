680 - What is the importance of rivers and lakes in human civilization?

Rivers and lakes play a crucial role in human civilization.

  1. Water supply: Rivers and lakes provide people with drinking water and water for farming and industries.
  2. Food source: Many people depend on fish and other seafood from rivers and lakes as a food source.
  3. Transportation: Rivers and lakes help transport goods and people from one place to another, which is important for trade.
  4. Energy source: Rivers can be used to generate electricity, which is a clean and renewable energy source.
  5. Recreation: Rivers and lakes offer places for people to swim, boat, and enjoy other recreational activities.

In short, rivers and lakes are vital for our survival and well-being.
