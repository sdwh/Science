715 - What role does transportation play in emergency response?

In an emergency, transportation plays a crucial role in getting help to people quickly and safely. Here are some ways transportation helps:

  1. Ambulances: They rush injured people to hospitals for medical care.
  2. Fire trucks: They take firefighters and equipment to fires or accidents to put out fires and rescue people.
  3. Emergency helicopters: They can fly over traffic to reach remote areas or transport patients to hospitals quickly.
  4. Disaster response vehicles: They carry food, water, and supplies to people affected by natural disasters.
  5. Police cars: They help keep people safe by controlling traffic and responding to emergencies.

Transportation helps emergency responders get to where they're needed quickly, so they can save lives and prevent more harm.
