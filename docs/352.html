Science Common Sense

352 - How do human activities impact ecosystems?

Human activities can harm or help ecosystems. Here are some examples:

Harmful activities:

  1. Pollution: Throwing trash, releasing chemicals, and using cars can dirty the air, water, and soil.
  2. Cutting trees: Destroying forests can lead to loss of homes for animals and make the air worse.
  3. Overfishing: Taking too many fish can hurt the ocean's balance and make it harder for other animals to survive.
  4. Climate change: Burning fossil fuels and releasing greenhouse gases can make the planet warmer and change the weather.

Helpful activities:

  1. Conservation: Protecting and planting trees, creating parks, and preserving habitats can help animals and plants thrive.
  2. Recycling: Reusing and recycling materials can reduce waste and help keep the environment clean.
  3. Sustainable living: Using renewable energy, reducing water use, and eating locally grown food can help reduce our impact on the environment.

We can all make a difference by choosing activities that help ecosystems and reducing those that harm them.
