1534 - What are the causes, effects, and prevention methods of acid rain

I'll explain acid rain in simple terms.

What is acid rain? Acid rain is rain that contains too much acid, making it bad for the environment. It's caused by pollutants in the air, like those from cars and factories, mixing with rainwater.

Causes of acid rain: 1. Burning fossil fuels (like coal, oil, and gas) for energy releases pollutants into the air. 2. Factories and cars release chemicals like sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides, which mix with rainwater and create acid rain. 3. Volcanic eruptions can also release these pollutants.

Effects of acid rain: 1. Acid rain harms plants and animals by changing the pH of lakes, rivers, and soil. 2. It can kill fish and other aquatic life. 3. Acid rain can damage buildings, statues, and monuments by eroding the stones. 4. It can also affect human health by polluting the air and water.

Prevention methods: 1. Reduce pollution: We can reduce pollution by using cleaner energy sources like solar and wind power. 2. Use public transport or carpool: Fewer cars on the road mean less pollution. 3. Plant trees: Trees help absorb pollutants and produce oxygen. 4. Recycle: Recycling helps reduce waste and saves natural resources. 5. Use eco-friendly products: Choose products that are designed to be environmentally friendly.

Remember, every small action we take can help reduce acid rain and protect our planet!
