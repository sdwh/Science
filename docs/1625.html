1625 - What factors create Taiwan's biodiversity?

Taiwan has a lot of different plants and animals, and that's because of several factors. Here are some of the main reasons:

  1. Location: Taiwan is an island, and it's located between Asia and the Pacific Ocean. This makes it a meeting point for animals and plants from different parts of the world.
  2. Mountains: Taiwan has lots of tall mountains, including some that are over 3,000 meters high. This creates different climates and habitats for plants and animals to live in.
  3. Weather: Taiwan has a special weather pattern, with hot and humid summers, and cool and rainy winters. This makes it a great place for plants to grow and animals to thrive.
  4. Volcanic soil: Taiwan is a volcanic island, and its soil is rich in nutrients. This helps plants grow strong and healthy.
  5. Protection from humans: Some areas in Taiwan are protected from human development, like national parks and nature reserves. This helps keep the natural habitats healthy and safe for plants and animals.

All these factors combined make Taiwan a perfect place for different plants and animals to live and thrive.
