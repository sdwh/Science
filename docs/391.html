How do temperature and precipitation influence ecosystems?

Temperature and precipitation are two important factors that affect ecosystems.

  1. Temperature:
  2. It determines the types of plants and animals that can live in a place (some species only live in cold or warm temperatures).
  3. It influences the growth of plants, affecting how fast they grow or if they can survive at all.

  4. It affects how much energy animals have and how active they can be.

  5. Precipitation (Rain or snow):

  6. It supplies water for plants and animals, crucial for survival.
  7. It determines the type of plants and trees that can grow in a place (some plants need a lot of water).
  8. It creates habitats like rivers, lakes, or wetlands, where animals can live.

Think of it like your own garden: if you don't water it (precipitation), the plants will die. And if it's too hot or cold (temperature), they also won't grow well. Both temperature and precipitation are essential for a balanced ecosystem.
