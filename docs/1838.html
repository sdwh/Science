1838 - How can geological sensitive areas be confirmed?

To confirm geological sensitive areas, scientists use a few methods:

  1. Mapping: They create detailed maps of the area to identify unique landforms, rocks, and soil types.
  2. Satellite Imagery: They study satellite images to see the area from above and spot signs of geological activity, like landslides or earthquakes.
  3. Field Observations: They go to the area and observe the rocks, soil, and landforms up close to collect more information.
  4. Drilling and Sampling: They drill into the ground to collect rock and soil samples, which are then studied in a lab to learn more about the area's geology.
  5. Ground-penetrating Radar and Other Tools: They use special tools to "see" underground structures, like caves or hidden faults, without digging.

By combining these methods, scientists can confirm if an area is geologically sensitive and needs special care to protect it.
