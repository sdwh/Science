What causes different weather patterns?

Weather patterns are caused by the movement of air and water on Earth. Here's a simplified explanation:

  1. Sun's heat: The sun heats the Earth's surface, warming the air closest to the ground.
  2. Air rises: As the air warms, it rises, creating low pressure near the ground.
  3. Air moves in: Cooler air from nearby moves in to fill the empty space, creating wind.
  4. Moisture forms clouds: As air rises, it cools, and the water vapor in the air turns into clouds.
  5. Climate and geography: The Earth's shape, mountains, oceans, and temperature differences affect how air and water move, creating different weather patterns.

Think of it like a big, natural circulation system that's always moving and changing.
