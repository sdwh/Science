1794 - What are the possibilities and limitations of emerging energy and alternative energy development in Taiwan.

Emerging energy and alternative energy development in Taiwan has both possibilities and limitations. Here are some key points to consider:

Possibilities:

  1. Renewable energy growth: Taiwan has a lot of sunshine, wind, and water, making it perfect for solar, wind, and hydro power. These clean energies can help reduce Taiwan's dependence on fossil fuels.
  2. Offshore wind farms: Taiwan's coastline is long and suitable for building large offshore wind farms, which can generate a lot of electricity.
  3. Geothermal energy: Taiwan is located near a volcano, which makes it a good place for geothermal energy. This energy can be used to generate electricity and heat water.

Limitations:

  1. Energy storage: Alternative energy sources like solar and wind are not always available when they're needed. Taiwan needs to develop better energy storage systems to save excess energy for later use.
  2. High costs: Building alternative energy infrastructure can be expensive. Taiwan needs to invest more money in these projects to make them affordable.
  3. Space constraints: Taiwan is a small island, and finding enough space for large-scale alternative energy projects can be challenging.
  4. Nuclear energy phase-out: Taiwan has decided to phase out nuclear energy, which means it needs to find alternative energy sources to replace it. This can be a big challenge.

Overall, Taiwan has a lot of potential for emerging energy and alternative energy development, but it also faces some challenges that need to be addressed.
