263 - How does urbanization change landscapes?

Urbanization changes landscapes in several ways:

  1. Buildings and roads: Cities need more buildings and roads, which replace natural habitats like forests and fields.
  2. Parks and green spaces disappear: To make room for development, parks and green spaces get destroyed or reduced in size.
  3. Changes in water flows: Urban areas often pave over natural waterways, which can cause flooding and water pollution.
  4. Loss of wildlife habitats: Urbanization can force animals to leave their homes and find new places to live, which can be difficult for them.
  5. Air and noise pollution: Cities have more vehicles and industrial activities, which contribute to air and noise pollution.

Overall, urbanization changes landscapes by replacing natural areas with human-made structures and activities.
