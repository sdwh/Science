Why is it important to think about consequences before acting?

Thinking about consequences before acting is important because it helps you make better choices and avoid problems.

Imagine you're playing with a ball near a window. If you throw the ball too hard, it might break the window. If you think about what might happen before throwing, you can decide to throw the ball softer or in a different direction. This helps you avoid breaking the window and getting in trouble.

Thinking about consequences helps you:

  1. Make better choices
  2. Avoid problems
  3. Stay safe
  4. Be more responsible

So, always think before you act, and ask yourself, "What might happen if I do this?"
