1596 - How do animal hormones regulate physiological functions?

Hormones are like chemical messengers in animals, including humans. They're produced by special glands and help our body work properly. Here's how they regulate functions:

  1. Growth: Hormones help you grow taller and stronger by telling your body what to do.
  2. Mood: Some hormones control how you feel, like happiness or sadness.
  3. Digestion: Hormones tell your body how to break down food and use the energy.
  4. Energy: Hormones help you stay awake or asleep, and give you energy when you need it.
  5. Reproduction: Hormones help animals (including humans) reproduce and have babies.

When hormones are sent to different parts of the body, they give instructions on what to do. This helps keep everything working smoothly and keeps you healthy!
