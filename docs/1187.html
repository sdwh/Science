1187 - What are the main natural disasters in Taiwan and how to recognize and prevent them.

In Taiwan, some of the main natural disasters are:

  1. Earthquakes: Taiwan is near the edge of two big tectonic plates, so earthquakes can happen often.
  2. Signs to recognize: shaking, rumbling noise, objects falling down.

  3. Prevention:

    • During an earthquake, drop under a sturdy table and cover your head with your arms.
    • After an earthquake, be careful of aftershocks and fallen objects.

  4. Typhoons: Taiwan is hit by strong storms every year, which can bring strong winds and heavy rain.

  5. Signs to recognize: high winds, heavy rain, flash flooding.

  6. Prevention:

    • Stay indoors and avoid traveling during a typhoon.
    • Board up windows and bring outdoor objects inside.

  7. Landslides and Mudslides: Heavy rain can cause the ground to slide and block roads.

  8. Signs to recognize: loud rumbling noise, fast-moving mud, falling trees.

  9. Prevention:

    • Avoid traveling during heavy rain.
    • If you see a landslide or mudslide happening, leave the area quickly.

  10. Flooding: Heavy rain can cause rivers to overflow and flood nearby areas.

  11. Signs to recognize: fast-moving water, rising water levels.

  12. Prevention:

    • Avoid traveling during heavy rain.
    • If you see floodwaters rising, leave the area quickly.

  13. Drought: Taiwan can have periods of very little rain, causing water shortages.

  14. Signs to recognize: low water levels in reservoirs, dry crops.
  15. Prevention:
    • Conserve water during a drought.
    • Don't waste water when brushing teeth or washing hands.

Remember: knowing the signs of these natural disasters and how to prevent them can help keep you safe. Always listen to authorities and follow their advice.
