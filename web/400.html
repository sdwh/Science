How do tides work, and what causes them?

Imagine the Earth is like a big ball, and the ocean is the water around it. The moon and the sun are like two big friends that pull on the water with their gravity.

When the moon is close to a part of the ocean, it pulls the water towards itself, making a 'bulge' in the ocean. The same thing happens on the other side of the Earth, but it's not as strong because the moon is far away.

The sun also pulls on the water, but not as much as the moon. When the sun and moon are on the same side of the Earth, they pull together and make the bulges even bigger, which creates higher 'high tides.'

When they are on opposite sides, they pull in different directions, making 'lower high tides.' The movement between these two types of tides is what we call the rise and fall of the ocean's tides.
