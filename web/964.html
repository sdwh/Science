How do governments and communities prepare for and respond to disasters?

Governments and communities prepare for and respond to disasters in several ways:

  1. Creating Emergency Plans: They make plans that include steps to take during and after a disaster. This plan includes evacuation routes, emergency shelters, and communication methods.

  2. Conducting Drills and Training: They practice disaster drills so people know what to do in case of a disaster.

  3. Stockpiling Supplies: They collect food, water, and medicine to help people affected by the disaster.

  4. Building Disaster-Resistant Buildings: They construct buildings and homes that can withstand disasters like hurricanes or earthquakes.

  5. Monitoring Weather and Hazards: They watch the weather and detect any potential hazards so they can warn people before a disaster strikes.

  6. Providing Emergency Services: During a disaster, they send emergency services like firefighters, police, and medical teams to help people.

  7. Rebuilding and Recovery: After a disaster, they work to rebuild and restore damaged areas and help people recover from the disaster.
