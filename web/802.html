What are some modern technologies used in farming today?

Modern farming uses many cool technologies to make farming easier and more efficient. Here are a few examples:

  1. GPS tractors: Tractors with GPS help farmers plant seeds and spray fertilizers in the right places, reducing waste.
  2. Drones: Drones fly over fields to take photos and videos, helping farmers find problems like weeds or pests before they become big issues.
  3. Robotics: Robots can automate tasks like harvesting and sorting crops, making farming faster and easier.
  4. Precision irrigation: Sensors and computers help farmers water their crops just the right amount, saving water and energy.
  5. Vertical farming: This method grows plants in vertical stacks, using less land and water to grow more food.
  6. Apps and software: Farming apps help farmers track their crops, weather, and soil health, making it easier to make informed decisions.

These technologies help farmers grow more food, use fewer resources, and take care of the environment.
