How do ecosystems provide services to humans?

Ecosystems provide services to humans in many ways. Some of these ways include:

  1. Air and Water Cleaning: Trees and plants help clean the air we breathe, and wetlands and oceans help clean the water we drink.
  2. Food Production: Forests, farms, and oceans give us food like fruits, vegetables, and fish.
  3. Climate Regulation: Forests and oceans help regulate the Earth's temperature, which keeps our climate stable.
  4. Medicine Discovery: Many medicines come from plants and animals found in ecosystems.
  5. Natural Protection: Ecosystems like coral reefs and mangroves protect us from storms and erosion.
  6. Recreation and Tourism: Ecosystems like parks and beaches provide places for us to play, relax, and have fun.
  7. Soil Formation: Ecosystems like forests help create soil, which is necessary for growing food.

These are just a few examples of the many ways ecosystems help humans.
